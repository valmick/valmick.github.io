# Introduction

## Symptoms
1. Behind on Language skills
2. Sometimes doesn't respond to his name
3. Lack of feeling pain when hurt
4. When focussed on activity like his mixer grinder he will not respond
5. He likes textures, so for example a book with a velvet texture,
   he will hold it and keep stroking it, he also likes velvet
   bedsheets. 
6. Throwing trantrums or having meltdowns
   1. Take him away from water
   2. Take him away from the sand
   3. Take him away from his mixer grinder
7. Sometimes does a repetitive hand movement like Rajnikant flipping
   his specs
8. He is not friendly to all.. he chooses his favorites and it takes
   him a lot of time to get to know someone to be able to mingle
   with that person

### Coping Activities

#### Walk
Describe more things that interest him
- Describe car (color/size/share/etc)
- Describe the dog
- Describe the trees
- Describe the flowers
  
#### Water wading pool
- Tell story about fishes, boats

#### Build a fort
- Secret place to eating a snack
- Read books which they like

#### hide objects 
- Not too many
- Point towards it
- Or use eyes to show it
- Or use words
- Use words such as behind the couch
- Use words such as left, right

#### Bubbles
- see if they can blow it
- make them ask for it
- try to face to face interaction

#### People games
- Pay attention with joint activity
- They should respond to and communicate during the activity
  - Row Row your boat
  - Head Shoulder knees and toes
  [Handout with pages on games](https://www.actcommunity.ca/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/Strategies-for-Supporting-Young-Children-April-30-handout.pdf)

#### Help
